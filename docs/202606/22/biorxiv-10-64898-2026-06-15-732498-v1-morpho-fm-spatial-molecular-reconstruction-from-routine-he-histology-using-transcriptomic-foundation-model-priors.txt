Abstract 14 

Routine haematoxylin and eosin (H&E) histology captures tissue architecture at clinical 15 

scale, but lacks a direct molecular readout of the transcriptional programmes that 16 

organise tumour epithelium, stroma, vasculature and immune compartments.  Spatial 17 

transcriptomics provides this context, yet cost, workflow complexity and sparse 18 

sampling limit routine use.  Most existing histology -to -expression models are trained de 19 

novo on small paired cohorts and therefore remain weakly constrained when 20 

extrapolating from sparse measurements to dense, tissue -wide molecular maps.  Here 21 

we introduce Morpho -FM, a weakly supervised framework that predicts spatial gene 22 

expression from routine H&E whole -slide images by conditioning a pretrained single -23 

cell transcriptomic foundation -model prior on local histological neighbourhoods.  A24 

lightweight morphology -to -transcriptome adapter maps cached whole -slide histology 25 

features into a transcriptomic decoder, enabling prediction at measured locations, dense 26 

full -section reconstruction, and re -aggregation to the original measurement support .27

overall performance among five representative methods, reaching mean per -gene 29

Introduction 43 

Routine haematoxylin and eosin (H&E) histology remains the most widely deployed 44 

assay for tumour diagnosis and tissue assessment, because it captures cellular 45 

morphology, stromal organization and tissue architecture at whole -slide scale  1. Yet 46 

H&E images provide only an indirect view of the molecular programmes that shape 47 

tumour epithelium, stroma, vasculature and immune compartments  2. Transcriptional 48 

states that influence progression, therapy response and tissue microenvironmental 49 

organization often remain hidden behind morphologically similar regions.  Bridging this 50 

gap between routine histology and spatially resolved molecular characterization has 51 

therefore become a central goal of computational pathology and spatial omics  3–6.52 

Spatial transcriptomics (ST) provides a direct route to this molecular context by 53 

measuring gene expression within native tissue architecture  7–9. These technologies 54 

have revealed spatial tumour states, tissue boundaries, immune niches and multicellular 55 

programmes that cannot be recovered from dissociated sequencing alone. However, 56 

their routine use remains limited by cost, workflow complexity, variable resolution and 57 

incomplete sampling of large tissue sections  9,10 . In many practical settings, only sparse 58 

spatial measurements are available, whereas neighbouring or archived H&E sections 59 

are abundant. A scalable framework that can use sparse spatial transcriptomic 60 

supervision to molecularly reannotate routine histology would therefore expand the 61 

reach of spatial omics beyond directly assayed locations  11 .62

This need has motivated models that predict spatial gene expression from H&E images .63 

Existing approaches have shown that histology contains measurable transcriptomic 64 

signal, but most are trained de novo on small paired cohorts and treat gene expression 65 

as a high -dimensional output vector to be learned almost entirely from local image 66 

supervision  12 –17 . This design leaves the output space weakly constrained, even though 67 

real expression profiles are structured by co -expression relationships, cell -state 68 

programmes and biological dependencies across genes.  This limitation is particularly 69 

relevant in cancer, where local histomorphology reflects not only cell -intrinsic 70 

transcriptional states but also spatially contextual interactions within the tumour 71 

microenvironment that may not be adequately captured from limited paired examples 72 

alone .73 

Two classes of foundation model have advanced rapidly but remain largely 74 

disconnected in the task of inferring spatial gene expression from histology. In 75 

computational pathology, pretrained whole -slide encoders can represent tissue 76 

morphology at clinical scale  18 –20 . In single -cell genomics, transcriptomic foundation 77 

models have learned the structure of gene -expression space, including co -expression 78 

patterns, cell -state programmes and gene –gene dependencies  21,22 . Existing histology -79 

to -expression models have mainly asked whether stronger image representations can 80 

improve prediction from limited paired H&E -ST data. A more consequential question 81 

is whether morphology should be decoded through a transcriptomic foundation -model 82 

prior that constrains the space of biologically plausible expression states. In this view, 83 

pathology -derived representations identify local tissue context, while a single -cell 84 

transcriptomic prior restricts molecular decoding to states compatible with that context. 85 

We reasoned that coupling these two priors could address a central limitation of sparse 86 

paired datasets: learning not only where morphology varies across a tissue section, but 87 

also which transcriptional states, spatial distributions and tumour –microenvironment 88 

interactions those variations are likely to reflect .89 

Here we introduce Morpho -FM, a weakly supervised framework for spatial molecular 90 

reconstruction from routine H&E whole -slide images using transcriptomic foundation -91 

model priors. Morpho -FM represents each measured spatial location as a local 92 

histological neighbourhood, maps cached whole -slide morphology features into the 93 

CellFM  23  transcriptomic latent space and decodes gene expression under sparse spatial 94 

transcriptomic supervision. The same trained model supports prediction at measured 95 

locations, dense full -section reconstruction and re -aggregation to the original 96

Results 101 

Morpho -FM connects pathology -scale morphology to a transcriptomic 102 

foundation -model decoder 103 

Morpho -FM was designed to test whether the pathology and transcriptomic foundation -104 

model streams described above can be coupled for spatial molecular prediction.  Rather 105 

than predicting each gene directly from an isolated H&E patch, the framework uses 106 

local histological neighbourhoods as the conditioning signal and decodes them through 107 

a pretrained single -cell transcriptomic foundation -model prior. This design separates 108 

the prediction problem into two coupled constraints: pathology -derived features capture 109 

where tissue architecture varies across the section, whereas the transcriptomic decoder 110 

constrains which expression states are plausible at those locations. To support both 111 

prediction at measured locations and dense full -section reconstruction, Morpho -FM 112 

implements this coupling within a shared coordinate system that links measured ST 113 

locations, cached whole -slide histology features, and dense prediction coordinates over 114 

the tissue mask  (Fig. 1a) .115 

The workflow begins by converting each raw H&E whole -slide image into cached 116 

whole -slide histology features using a Hierarchical Image Pyramid Transformer (HIPT) 117  

> 19

encoder as the histology feature backbone (Fig. 1a). These features provide a reusable 118 

morphological representation for both spot -supervised training and dense decoding. 119 

Because measured assay locations and unmeasured tissue regions are indexed within 120 

the same spatial reference, Morpho -FM avoids constructing separate representations 121 

for spot -level prediction and tissue -wide reconstruction. Dense outputs can therefore be 122 

re -aggregated to the original measurement support, enabling quantitative consistency 123 

checking rather than serving only as qualitative super -resolution maps. 124 

For sparse ST supervision, each measured location is represented by a local disk -shaped 125 

multiple -instance learning (MIL) bag sampled from the cached feature grid (Fig. 1b). 126 

Grid positions within the spot -radius neighbourhood are selected as instance features, 127 

so that each measurement is associated with its surrounding histological context rather 128

A lightweight morphology -to -transcriptome adapter then maps the instance features 132 

from each MIL bag into a transcriptomic decoder implemented with CellFM  23 , a133 

pretrained single -cell transcriptomic foundation model (Fig. 1c).  The decoder produces 134 

instance -level expression rates, which are mean -aggregated to obtain a bag -level 135 

prediction aligned with the original ST measurement support. Thus, Morpho -FM does 136 

not simply append a regression head to a pathology encoder.  Instead, i t inserts a 137 

transcriptomic foundation model at the molecular readout stage, so that morphology -138 

derived signals are interpreted within an expression space shaped by gene -gene 139 

dependencies and cell -state structure learned from large -scale single -cell data. 140 

After spot -supervised training, Morpho -FM can be used in two complementary modes: 141 

prediction at measured locations and dense full -section reconstruction over the tissue 142 

mask (Fig. 1a). The resulting continuous expression fields can be visualized as tissue -143 

wide molecular maps or re -aggregated to the original measurement support for 144 

comparison with measured ST data. We therefore organized the Results as a sequence 145 

of tests of the central hypothesis: first, whether this coupled decoder improves cross -146 

section prediction; second, whether the gain is attributable to the transcriptomic 147 

foundation -model prior; and third, whether the resulting predictions and latent 148 

representations recover interpretable molecular tissue architecture beyond benchmark -149 

level predictive performance .150

Pretrained CellFM initialization is the dominant controlled source of Morpho -FM 293 

performance gain 294 

The benchmark results were consistent with the hypothesis that a transcriptomic 295 

foundation -model decoder improves morphology -conditioned expression prediction, 296 

but benchmark comparisons alone do not identify the source of this advantage. We 297 

therefore isolated the contribution of pretrained transcriptomic initialization by 298 

comparing Morpho -FM models initialized with either pretrained CellFM weights or 299 

randomly initialized CellFM weights under matched multi -slide and single -slide 300 

prostate training settings. All remaining implementation details were controlled as 301 

described in Supplementary Note 2, enabling a direct test of whether pretrained single -302 

cell transcriptomic priors improve prediction when training data, model architecture 303 

and optimization settings are otherwise held fixed. 304 

Pretrained CellFM initialization improved prediction in both matched training settings, 305 

as measured by mean per -gene Pearson correlation and supported by per -gene 306 

correlation distributions and Pearson -threshold curves (Fig. 4a,b). In the multi -slide 307 

setting, the pretrained -initialized Morpho -FM model achieved a mean per -gene Pearson 308 

correlation of 0.360, compared with 0.299 for random initialization, an absolute 309 

increase of  0.061. In the single -slide setting, the corresponding values were 0.346 and 310 

0.265,  an absolute increase of 0.081 . The larger gain in the single -slide comparison 311 

indicates that pretrained transcriptomic structure  is especially beneficial when paired 312 

spatial transcriptomic supervision is sparse, supporting its role as a constraint on 313 

morphology -conditioned expression prediction .314 

Expression -stratified analysis showed that the gain from pretrained transcriptomic 315 

initialization was not a uniform offset across all genes. Gains were small among low -316 

expression genes, for which per -gene correlations remained low across the pretrained 317 

and randomly initialized models in both training settings, but increased in the mid - and 318 

high -expression strata (Fig. 4c). This pattern was reproduced in both the multi -slide and 319 

single -slide settings, with the largest improvement observed for high -expression genes 320 

in the data -limited single -slide comparison. A fixed prostate marker panel provided a 321 

gene -level view of the same effect: pretrained initialization improved correlations for 322 

several markers, including  AR , AMACR , ERG , EPCAM  and  KRT8 , whereas  KLK3 323 

showed near -ceiling correlations across all model variants (Pearson r ≈ 0.99; Fig. 4d). 324

We next asked whether a comparable gain could be obtained by changing the histology 327 

feature backbone while keeping the pretrained transcriptomic decoder fixed. Replacing 328 

HIPT features with ImageNet -pretrained ResNet -50 features produced comparable 329 

mean per -gene Pearson correlations in both prostate settings: ResNet -50 was slightly 330 

higher in the single -slide comparison, whereas HIPT was slightly higher in the multi -331 

slide comparison (Supplementary Fig. 11). Expression -stratified summaries, Pearson -332 

threshold curves and marker -panel correlations similarly showed no stable advantage 333 

for either histology feature backbone. 334 

These controls do not indicate that histology features are unimportant; local 335 

morphology remains the conditioning signal for all Morpho -FM predictions. Rather, 336 

they separate two possible explanations for the benchmark gains. Within the present 337 

Morpho -FM design, replacing the histology feature backbone did not provide a 338 

consistent additional gain, whereas pretrained transcriptomic initialization produced a 339 

reproducible improvement in both data -rich and data -limited settings. The ablations 340 

therefore support the central design claim: in sparse H&E -ST learning, constraining the 341 

molecular decoder with a transcriptomic foundation -model prior is a clearer and more 342 

reproducible source of improvement than simply changing the upstream image feature 343 

backbone. 344

Discussion 532 

Morpho -FM addresses a central unresolved problem at the interface of computational 533 

pathology and spatial omics: whether routine H&E morphology can be decoded 534 

through a transcriptomic foundation -model prior to recover spatial molecular states 535 

when paired H&E -ST data are limited. The main contribution of this study is therefore 536 

not simply another histology -to -expression predictor, but a framework that connects 537 

two previously parallel foundation -model streams. Pathology foundation models 538 

provide scalable representations of tissue architecture, whereas single -cell 539 

transcriptomic foundation models encode structured constraints over gene -expression 540 

states. By inserting the transcriptomic foundation model at the molecular readout stage, 541 

Morpho -FM allows morphology -derived signals to be interpreted within a biologically 542 

constrained expression space rather than through an unconstrained regression head. 543 

Across prostate and kidney cancer benchmarks, external clear -cell renal cell carcinoma 544 

transfer, Xenium breast cancer reconstruction and HER2ST cross -platform analysis, 545 

this coupling improved prediction and supported spatial molecular reannotation of 546 

routine histology. 547 

The ablation experiments sharpen this interpretation. Pretrained histology encoders are 548 

valuable because local morphology remains the conditioning signal for every prediction, 549 

but changing the upstream histology feature backbone did not produce a stable 550 

independent gain in the matched prostate controls. In contrast, pretrained CellFM 551 

initialization reproducibly improved prediction, with the larger gain observed in the 552 

single -slide setting where paired supervision was most limited. This result suggests that, 553 

> .CC-BY-NC 4.0 International license made available under a (which was not certified by peer review) is the author/funder, who has granted bioRxiv a license to display the preprint in perpetuity. It is The copyright holder for this preprint this version posted June 19, 2026. ;https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.06.15.732498 doi: bioRxiv preprint

under sparse H&E -ST supervision, the dominant bottleneck is not only how tissue 554 

morphology is represented, but also how the molecular output space is constrained. 555 

Treating gene expression as a high -dimensional vector learned de novo from a small 556 

number of paired sections forces the model to rediscover co -expression structure, 557 

expression distributions and cell -state dependencies from limited data. Morpho -FM 558 

instead transfers part of this structure from a pretrained single -cell transcriptomic model, 559 

helping stabilize morphology -conditioned decoding. 560 

This design also reframes dense histology -to -expression prediction. Dense molecular 561 

maps from H&E are often visually compelling, but unmeasured positions do not have 562 

direct ground truth. We therefore linked dense full -section decoding to the original 563 

measurement support through re -aggregation, so that tissue -wide molecular 564 

reconstruction remained anchored to quantitative evaluation. In Xenium breast cancer 565 

sections, Morpho -FM recovered  ERBB2 -rich tumour compartments, preserved marker 566 

programmes spanning epithelial, stromal, vascular, proliferative and immune categories, 567 

and retained boundary - and ROI -level molecular trends after re -aggregation. The latent -568 

domain analyses further showed that pre -readout Morpho -FM representations aligned 569 

more closely with curated tissue compartments than raw morphology features alone and 570 

recovered  KRT14 /KRT5 -enriched myoepithelial structures around DCIS regions. These 571 

findings indicate that the transcriptomic decoder contributes not only gene -wise 572 

accuracy, but also a latent organization of tissue that is more compatible with molecular 573 

compartment structure. 574 

At the same time, the results define the appropriate scope of the framework. Morpho -575 

FM does not replace spatial transcriptomics, and its dense fields should not be 576 

interpreted as directly measured expression. The method still requires paired H&E and 577 

spatial transcriptomic data for training, and it inherits sequencing noise, registration 578 

uncertainty, gene -panel limitations and platform -specific biases from those 579 

measurements. Re -aggregation to the original measurement support provides an 580 

important consistency check, but it cannot validate every unmeasured grid position. 581 

Similarly, latent domains and HER2ST candidate domains should be regarded as 582 

hypotheses for marker -guided inspection, orthogonal validation or pathological review, 583 

rather than independently established tissue classes. The reduced performance in one 584 

external ccRCC section also emphasizes that cross -dataset transfer remains sensitive to 585 

tissue heterogeneity, preprocessing differences and platform effects. 586 

These limitations point to several priorities for future development. First, broader multi -587

regression toward foundation -model -constrained molecular decoding from routine 600 

histology. By coupling pathology -scale morphology with a single -cell transcriptomic 601 

prior, the framework uses sparse spatial transcriptomic supervision to extend molecular 602 

information across H&E sections while preserving a clear distinction between 603 

measured data and model -inferred maps. This makes Morpho -FM relevant for 604 

molecular reannotation of pathology archives, selection of regions for orthogonal 605 

profiling and exploration of spatial gene -expression programmes in tissue regions that 606 

were not directly assayed. More broadly, the study suggests that the next step for 607 

computational pathology may not be only larger image encoders, but architectures that 608 

connect visual tissue structure to pretrained biological state spaces. 609

Model training and checkpoint selectio n717 

All Morpho -FM models were trained using AdamW optimization with separate 718 

learning -rate groups for the transcriptomic decoder parameters and the morphology -to -719 

transcriptome adapter and dispersion parameters. Training used automatic mixed 720 

precision, early stopping on validation negative -binomial loss and retention of the 721 

checkpoint with the lowest validation loss for all downstream inference and evaluation. 722 

The default training configuration used 100 maximum epochs, a batch size of four 723 

measurement locations, a weight decay of  10 −2 , an early -stopping patience of five 724 

epochs and a random seed of 42. Gene chunk size was set to 256 genes for large panels 725 

and was increased accordingly for smaller panels to ensure full coverage within fewer 726 

forward passes. Complete hyperparameters, learning -rate schedules and 727 

implementation details are provided in Supplementary Note 8. 728 

The inference workflow accepted an explicit checkpoint and target section as inputs, 729 

enabling both within -section dense reconstruction and cross -section transfer without 730 

modifying the training procedure or the dense -decoding pipeline .731

Prostate benchmark protocol 753 

The prostate benchmark compared Morpho -FM with five representative histology -to -754 

expression methods: HisToGene, iStar, mclSTExp, sCellST and HiST. Four HEST 755 

prostate cancer sections, INT25, INT26, INT27 and INT28, were used. Each section 756 

comprised an H&E whole -slide image, an ST count matrix, spatial measurement 757 

coordinates and assay scale -factor metadata. All methods were evaluated on the same 758 

sections, the same H&E -aligned spatial coordinates and the same 17,512 -gene panel. 759 

Prediction outputs from all methods were converted to a common spot -ordered and 760 

gene -ordered matrix before evaluation. Method -specific input conversion, validation 761 

handling and output alignment were implemented as needed, but the core architectures 762 

and loss functions of the baseline methods were not modified. 763 

Two prostate benchmark protocols were used. In the rotating single -slide protocol, each 764 

of the four prostate sections served once as the sole training slide, and the remaining 765 

three sections were held out for independent testing. This yielded 12 directed train –test 766 

comparisons. Validation in the single -slide setting was constructed within the training 767 

slide using a spatially blocked partition rather than a random spot split, reducing 768 

leakage from local spatial autocorrelation. In the rotating multi -slide protocol, two 769 

prostate sections were used for training, one section for validation and one section for 770 

held -out testing. The held -out test section was rotated across the four prostate sections, 771 

yielding four folds. The validation slide or blocked validation subset was used only for 772 

checkpoint selection and early stopping .773

Transcriptomic initialization and histology feature -backbone ablation s791 

To isolate the contribution of pretrained transcriptomic initialization, we performed a 792 

controlled  2 × 2 ablation crossing two training regimes with two initialization 793 

conditions. The training regimes were a multi -slide prostate setting and a single -slide 794

randomly initialized CellFM weights. All four branches used the same data partitions, 796

training budget, early -stopping procedure and inference pipeline. Performance gain 798 

from pretrained transcriptomic initialization was defined as the difference in the chosen 799 

evaluation metric between the pretrained and randomly initialized CellFM branches 800 

under the same training regime. 801

and multi -slide settings. This design allowed the ablation analysis to distinguish the 806
